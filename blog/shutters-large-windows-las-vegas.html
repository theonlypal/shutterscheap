Large Window Guide

Best Shutters for Large Windows in Las Vegas

Updated December 2024 • By the Shutters Cheap Team • 7 min read

Las Vegas homes are famous for their big, beautiful windows - great room picture windows, wall-to-wall sliders, and dramatic two-story glass that lets in stunning desert light. But those same windows can be a challenge when it comes to window treatments.

After 20+ years installing shutters on oversized windows throughout the Las Vegas valley, we've learned exactly what works (and what doesn't) for large window applications. Here's our complete guide to getting shutters right on your big windows.

Why Shutters Are Ideal for Large Windows

Before we dive into specifics, let's address why plantation shutters are often the best choice for large windows:

  • No sagging or warping: Unlike blinds with long cord spans, shutters maintain their structure
  • Better insulation: Critical for blocking Las Vegas heat on south-facing glass walls
  • Proportional appearance: Large louvers look natural on big windows
  • Increased home value: Shutters are permanent fixtures that appraisers value
  • Durability: No cords to break, no fabric to fade or tear

Large Window Shutter Configurations

Large windows require special panel configurations. Here's how we handle different scenarios:

Windows 36-60 Inches Wide

Typically use 2-4 panels in a bi-fold configuration. Panels fold to the sides when you want full light and view access.

Windows 60-96 Inches Wide

Usually configured with 4-6 panels. We often recommend a split configuration where panels fold to both sides, giving you centered access to the view.

Windows Over 96 Inches Wide

Extra-wide windows may use 6-8+ panels with multiple track systems. Bypass (sliding) configurations are sometimes better than bi-fold for extremely wide spans.

Choosing the Right Louver Size

Louver size matters more on large windows than on standard windows. Here's our recommendation:

4.5-Inch Louvers (Recommended for Large Windows)

  • Look proportional on oversized windows
  • Provide maximum view-through when open
  • Easier to clean (fewer slats)
  • Modern, contemporary aesthetic

3.5-Inch Louvers (Good Alternative)

  • Work well on medium-large windows
  • Slightly less expensive than 4.5"
  • Better light control (more positions)
  • Traditional plantation look
Pro Tip: On two-story great room windows, we almost always recommend 4.5" louvers. They look properly scaled from across the room and don't appear "busy" like smaller louvers can.

Sliding Glass Door Shutters

Sliding glass doors are one of the most common large window applications in Las Vegas homes. Here's what you need to know:

Bypass Track System

Panels slide on tracks like barn doors. Best for:

  • Doors you use frequently
  • Limited wall space for bi-fold panels
  • 8-foot and wider door spans

Bi-Fold System

Panels fold accordion-style to the sides. Best for:

  • Maximum access to the full opening
  • Doors you don't use daily
  • Wider wall space available

Material Considerations for Large Windows

Large windows put extra stress on shutter materials. Here's why material choice matters:

Poly Vinyl (Recommended)

  • No warping: Handles weight and heat without bending
  • Lightweight: Easier on hinges and tracks
  • Heat resistant: Critical for large south/west windows
  • 25-year warranty: Protection for your investment

Wood (Use With Caution)

  • Beautiful appearance but heavier
  • May warp on sun-exposed windows
  • Requires stronger hardware
  • Higher maintenance in desert climate
Las Vegas Tip: For large windows facing south or west, we strongly recommend poly vinyl over wood. The intense desert sun causes real wood to expand, contract, and potentially warp over time. Poly vinyl is engineered to handle 115Â°F+ temperatures.

Special Considerations by Room

Great Rooms & Living Rooms

  • Often have the largest windows in the home
  • Consider motorized tilt for hard-to-reach windows
  • 4.5" louvers look most proportional
  • Split configurations allow flexible light control

Master Bedrooms

  • Blackout capability important for shift workers
  • Consider room-darkening hidden tilt rods
  • Shutters block more light than blinds

Dining Rooms

  • Large windows often overlook patios/pools
  • Easy-clean surfaces important near food areas
  • Bi-fold for entertaining access

Installation Considerations

Large window installations require extra planning:

  • Structural support: Heavy shutters need properly anchored frames
  • Access: Some large windows require ladders or scaffolding
  • Delivery: Large panels may need special handling
  • Timeline: Custom large shutters typically take 4-6 weeks

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Choosing too-small louvers: 2.5" louvers look busy on large windows
  2. Skipping reinforcement: Wide panels need extra frame support
  3. DIY on large windows: Professional installation ensures proper alignment
  4. Ignoring sun exposure: Wrong material on sunny windows = warping
  5. Not testing operation: Always verify panels open/close smoothly
