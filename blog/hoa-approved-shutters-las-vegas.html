HOA Approved Shutters for Las Vegas Communities

Your complete guide to HOA-compliant window treatments in Summerlin, Henderson, and master-planned communities.

Updated December 2024 | By Shutters Cheap

Good News:

White and off-white plantation shutters are approved by virtually every HOA in Las Vegas. They're the #1 HOA-compliant choice because they look uniform from the street and add home value.

Understanding Las Vegas HOA Rules for Window Treatments

Las Vegas has more than 3,000 HOA-governed communities. Most were established by developers like Howard Hughes Corporation (Summerlin), American Nevada Corporation (Green Valley), and others who set CC&Rs (Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions) for neighborhood uniformity.

The good news: window treatment rules are typically straightforward. The goal is a uniform appearance from the street, not to restrict your choices inside.

General HOA Window Treatment Rules

Typically Allowed

  • White plantation shutters
  • Off-white/cream shutters
  • White or neutral blinds
  • White roller shades
  • Neutral drapes with white backing
  • Solar screens (most communities)

Typically Prohibited

  • Sheets, towels, or blankets
  • Aluminum foil on windows
  • Brightly colored blinds visible outside
  • Patterned curtains visible from street
  • Reflective films (without approval)
  • Mismatched treatments on same elevation

Community-Specific Guidelines

Summerlin

Summerlin Design Guidelines require window treatments to present a "neat and uniform appearance" from the exterior. White and off-white plantation shutters are specifically mentioned as acceptable in most village guidelines.

  • The Trails - White shutters approved
  • The Ridges - White or cream shutters approved
  • Red Rock Country Club - Neutral tones required
  • Summerlin South - White or off-white required

Henderson Communities

Henderson master-planned communities generally follow similar rules:

  • Anthem - White or neutral window treatments
  • Green Valley - Uniform appearance required
  • Seven Hills - Neutral colors from exterior
  • Inspirada - White or off-white
  • Lake Las Vegas - Check specific village rules

Southwest Las Vegas

  • Southern Highlands - White/neutral required
  • Mountains Edge - Uniform appearance
  • Rhodes Ranch - White or cream colors

How to Stay HOA Compliant

  1. Choose white or off-white - This is approved everywhere
  2. Use consistent treatments on each elevation - Don't mix shutters and blinds on the same wall of windows
  3. Keep louvers/slats horizontal - Standard for plantation shutters
  4. Check your specific CC&Rs - Usually available on your HOA's website
  5. When in doubt, ask - Email your HOA for written approval before ordering

Why Plantation Shutters Are the #1 HOA Choice

Plantation shutters dominate Las Vegas HOA communities because:

  • Uniform appearance - Look identical from the street, house to house
  • HOA-approved color - White is always acceptable
  • Increase home value - 3-5% according to real estate studies
  • Permanent installation - Unlike blinds that can droop or break
  • Premium perception - Buyers expect shutters in nice communities

Pro Tip:

If you're selling your home, plantation shutters are one of the few window treatment upgrades that return more than 100% of their cost. Buyers in HOA communities specifically look for homes with shutters already installed.
