2025 Energy Tax Credit for Shutters & Window Treatments

A complete guide for Las Vegas homeowners on which window treatments qualify for federal energy efficiency tax credits.

Updated December 2024 | By Shutters Cheap

Key Takeaway:

Standard plantation shutters don't qualify for tax credits on their own, but certain energy-efficient window treatments like cellular shades may qualify for up to $1,200 in federal tax credits annually.

Understanding the 2025 Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit

The Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit (formerly the Nonbusiness Energy Property Credit) was expanded under the Inflation Reduction Act and continues through 2032. It allows homeowners to claim 30% of the cost of qualifying energy-efficient improvements, up to $1,200 per year for certain categories.

Do Plantation Shutters Qualify for Tax Credits?

Here's the honest answer: Standard plantation shutters alone do not qualify for the federal energy tax credit. While our poly vinyl shutters block up to 73% of heat transfer and significantly reduce cooling costs in Las Vegas homes, they don't meet the specific ENERGY STAR certification requirements for the tax credit.

Some competitors advertise "tax credit eligible shutters," but this is often misleading. The IRS requires specific documentation and certification that most shutter products don't have.

Window Treatments That MAY Qualify

The following window treatments may qualify if they meet ENERGY STAR requirements:

Potentially Qualifying Products

  • Cellular/Honeycomb Shades - Must have documented R-values meeting ENERGY STAR thresholds
  • Insulated Drapes - With thermal backing and proper installation
  • Storm Windows - When replacing existing windows
  • Combination Systems - Shutters + cellular shades that together meet efficiency requirements

The Real Energy Savings for Las Vegas Homes

Even without tax credits, quality window treatments provide significant savings in Las Vegas:

Product Heat Reduction Est. Annual Savings
Poly Vinyl Shutters Up to 73% $200-$400/year
Cellular Shades Up to 60% $150-$300/year
Solar Screens Up to 90% $300-$600/year

NV Energy Rebates (Alternative Savings)

While federal tax credits may not apply to most shutters, check with NV Energy for local rebate programs. They occasionally offer rebates for energy-efficient window treatments that help reduce peak cooling demand.

How to Maximize Your Savings

  1. Combine products - Shutters + cellular shades provide both energy efficiency and style
  2. Focus on west-facing windows - These get the most afternoon sun in Las Vegas
  3. Consider solar screens - Block heat before it enters your home
  4. Consult a tax professional - Before claiming any credits
