Engineered For Las Vegas Desert Climate

Real wood shutters can struggle in Las Vegas. Our extreme heat (115°+ summers), intense UV exposure, and low humidity can cause wood to warp, crack, and fade. Polywood shutters are engineered specifically to thrive in these conditions.

Polywood is a premium poly vinyl material that delivers the elegant look of painted wood without any of the climate-related problems:

  • UV stable – Won't fade or yellow in intense sun
  • 100% waterproof – Perfect for bathrooms and kitchens
  • Won't crack or split – No dry air damage
  • Energy efficient – Superior insulation value
  • Low maintenance – Just wipe clean, no refinishing

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Polywood?

Polywood is a premium poly vinyl (composite) material engineered specifically for plantation shutters. It's made from a solid synthetic polymer that looks like painted wood but won't warp, crack, chip, or fade. It's waterproof and perfect for Las Vegas's extreme heat and dry climate.

Are Polywood shutters better than wood shutters in Las Vegas?

For most Las Vegas homes, yes. Polywood handles our extreme heat (115°+) and low humidity without warping or cracking—problems that affect real wood shutters. Polywood is also waterproof (ideal for bathrooms), more affordable, and comes with a longer warranty.

Do Polywood shutters look as good as real wood?

Modern Polywood shutters are virtually indistinguishable from painted wood shutters. They feature the same crisp lines, substantial feel, and elegant appearance. Most guests can't tell the difference—but your wallet and your peace of mind will notice the benefits.

How long do Polywood shutters last?

Polywood shutters are built to last 25+ years with minimal maintenance. We back every installation with a 25-year warranty covering warping, cracking, fading, and defects. They'll outlast several sets of blinds, curtains, or wood shutters.

How much do Polywood shutters cost in Las Vegas?

Polywood shutters typically cost $20-28 per square foot installed, making them 25-40% less expensive than real wood shutters. For a typical home, that's $250-$500 per window. We offer free in-home quotes and will beat any competitor's price by 10%.
