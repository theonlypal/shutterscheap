How Window Treatments Cut Your Cooling Costs

The insulating properties of quality window treatments directly impact energy consumption during our extended cooling season. Shutters create dead air space between the window glass and your room, which blocks heat transfer in both directions. This year-round benefit typically reduces cooling costs by twelve to eighteen percent in valley homes, with higher savings on particularly challenging exposures.

Solar screens provide even more dramatic cooling cost reductions on sun-exposed windows because they intercept heat before it reaches the glass. When solar radiation hits your window, it converts to heat. Solar screens stop this process eighteen inches outside your window, which means the heat never enters your home’s thermal envelope. Homeowners throughout established communities often discover that strategically combining different window treatment types achieves maximum savings while maintaining aesthetic appeal in homes throughout Summerlin’s master-planned neighborhoods and Henderson’s luxury developments that enhance both comfort and property value.

The cooling load reduction extends beyond the immediate window area. When your windows admit less heat, your air conditioning system runs shorter cycles to maintain comfortable temperatures. Over a typical twenty-year window treatment lifespan, these combined savings often exceed the initial installation cost several times over.
